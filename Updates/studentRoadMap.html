Road Map For The Students

After 12th ,a query usually arise in mind of every student by which subject should he/she graduate from? Choosing a graduation stream is not that simple however can be made simple. There are hundreds of degree, diploma and certificate options to choose from. When passing out of Class 12, the students usually have a number of questions in mind, such as:

  • Should I choose a specialised field right now or later?
  • Should I choose a popular course or an emerging field of study?
  • What exam(s) should I take to enrol in a certain course?

Before thinking about graduating subject , we need to know that in which field he /she take interest and in which field there is more scope in the future .Because we can easily do the work that we are interested in doing. And when it comes to our future, it becomes important to know .We should choose our graduation subject in which we are interested and which we can easily do.


To overcome this problem of children, we have given the names of some degrees below and their details:-
1.B.com :- B.Com stands for bachelor of commerce. It is the most popular undergraduate level course. B.Com programme design in a manner that it picks up concepts and principles covered in class 12th curriculum and offer in depth understanding about them to students. BCom or Bachelor of Commerce is a three-year undergraduate course imparted in regular as well as distance education mode. B.com is one of the most sought courses after 12th. It has the duration of 3 years divided into 6 semesters. The course covers a number of subjects during its study period. B.Com mainly focuses on Accounts, Mathematics and Economics. This course is designed to provide students with a wide range of managerial skills and understanding in streams like finance, accounting, taxation and management. The course is designed to provide students with a wide range of managerial skills and understanding in streams like finance, accounting, taxation and management.
Eligibility:
▶️Candidates having question “Am I eligible for pursuing Bachelor of Commerce (B.Com)?” can check the eligibility from this section:
▶️Candidates should have passed higher secondary examination with not less than 50% marks from a recognized board.
▶️Candidates having academic background in science, commerce and arts stream are eligible to apply for this course.

2.B.Sc:- BSc stands for bachelor of science .Bachelor of Science (BSc) is an undergraduate degree course usually of three years duration. It is one of the most popular course choices among Science students after Class 12th.this course has used as a foundation course for those students who want to make their future in science. BSc Physics, BSc Computer Science, BSc Chemistry, BSc Biology, BSc Mathematics, and so on are some popular BSc courses in that usually students want to pursue after class 12th.A BSc course can be pursued as both a full-time or part-time course. Students can choose to pursue plain BSc or BSc (Honours). The course is most apt for students who have a strong interest and background in Science and Mathematics. The course is also beneficial for students who wish to pursue multi and inter-disciplinary science careers in future. After the completion of a BSc degree, candidates can opt to pursue Master of Science (MSc) or even secure admission in a professional job-oriented course. BSc can be pursued in different Science subjects, some of the popular ones being Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, Computer Science, etc. Apart from this, candidates can also choose to pursue a BSc program course which focuses on all major subjects of Science. Eligibility:-Students should have cleared their class 12th exam from a recognised Board in Science stream with a minimum aggregate of 50% to 60%.

B.A.:- this graduation course stands for bachelor of arts. BA is an extremely popular undergraduate course that students pursue right after their class 12th. Bachelor of Arts or BA is an undergraduate program and it comes under the discipline of humanities/arts. BA course duration is 3 years. BA course is divided equally into 6 semesters. One having 10+2 degree in any stream can take up this course. There are various BA specialisations available namely Philosophy, Literature, Anthropology, Psychology, Theology, Journalism, French, Sanskrit etc and many more. BA course helps in enriching one’s outlook towards society and building it by the oldest forms of arts.

  • The eligibility criteria for a BA course specifies that candidates should have completed their 10+2 from a recognised board.
