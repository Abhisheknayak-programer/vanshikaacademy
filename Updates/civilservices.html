Civil Service Updates

What are civil services?
civil services are the bodies of government officials who are employed in civil occupations that are neither political nor judicial. India has parliamentary democracy. the mighty responsibility for running the administration rests with the people’s elected representatives-cabinet ministry,but a handful of ministers cannot be expected to deal personally with the problems of modern administration. Therefore ministers lay down the policy and it is for civil servants, who serve at the pleasure of the President of India to carry it out. Civil services are also described as the core permanent administrative arm of government that containing permanent and pensible officials working in government ministries, department and agencies. There is no exact definition for civil services because it includes stop would you advise on, develop and implement government policies and programmes and manage day to day activities.

What are civil servants
civil servants are the unsung heroes of Government and are the backbone of running a country. They works on the solving the problems that society faces they works in everything from health care and education to transport and energy.


How to become a civil servant


Any person who is Indian citizen can be a civil servant by cracking the competitive exam known as UPSC

UPSC stands for union public service commission which is an antonomous body that ensures unbiased recruitment to the civil services by conducting the examination. To take part in this examination age of candidates should be between 21 to 30 years. Age relaxation of 5 years and 3 years provide to those candidates belonging to St or SC and OBC categories respectively. And also candidate should have a bachelor’s degree in any discipline from any renowned University. To become an IAS IPS and Central services candidate must have to clear this competitive examination.

This exam is conducted in three phases:

  • Phase-1 this phase is called preliminary examination. It is a written examination and which held in the month of June every year. It contains two objective type papers.
  • Paper 1 :- this paper contain 150 questions. Each question endue one mark. This paper is called the general studies which consist 6 topics like geography, economy , science and current affair, Indian history e and constitution of India. Two hours are given to do this paper.
  • Paper 2 :- this paper is called optional paper. Because it is an optional paper you can choose any subject which you like most like political science, psychology , economics, history etc. It carries a score of 300 points on 120 questions and time duration of 2 hours given to do this paper. (Note:-it is tremendous competitive exam out of all candidates would take prelims only the highest scorers qualify for the second exam which is known as main exam. Success rate of this exam is less than 9%)



  • Phase- 2 this page is called main examination. It is written examination in subjective or as a type format. This exam is held in month of November or December. It contains 9 papers.
  • Paper 1 and 2 :-each paper contain 300 marks. These are language papers out of which one is English language paper compulsory and other is in any language mentioned in the 8th schedule of Indian constitution. Passing in this papers with a specified minimum percentage is necessary but the marks achieved in this paper are not added in the total score.
  • Paper 3 :- it contains 200 marks. It is NSA paper which can be right in either English or in any Indian language mentioned in 8th schedule of constitution.
  • Paper 4and5 :- each paper contained 300 marks and known as general studies. GK and current affairs related questions are come in this paper.
  • Paper 6,7,8and9 :- these are the four papers of two optional subjects. After 4th and 5th paper, candidate will have to take a optional subject which would contain two paper each. Each paper consists 300 marks.
  • Phase-3 :- personality test
    Expert panel measures the candidate’s personality. In this leadership, presence of mind, his/her social traits apart from their aptitude for work and capabilities are included. When a candidate clears the interview then his/her hard work pays off. He undergoes compulsory training at Lal Bahadur Shastri National academy of administration in mussoorie as a probationary officers.
  • All newly trained officers start as junior scale officer and move on to the higher levels like senior scale, additional secretary, chief secretary, cabinet secretary etc. Allowance and facilities given to the civil servants are commensurate with the job like housing, telephone, official vehicle, live travel concessions etc .
