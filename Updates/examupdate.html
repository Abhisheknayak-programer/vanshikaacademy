CBSE UPDATES

CBSE board examination update:
Central Board of Secondary Education, CBSE has issued a notice to affiliated schools in regards the Board Examinations 2021 evaluation. The board has asked schools to update teacher information on OASIS by April 10. The board has also warned schools penalty of upto Rs. 50,000 as well as a warning of no result declaration for the school in case of non-compliance. CBSE 10th 12th Board Exams 2021 are scheduled to begin from May 4. Also, the practical exams have already started and schools are required to complete the same between March 1, 2021 and June 11, 2021. However, soon after CBSE sent out lists of teachers for external examiner, it came to the notice of CBSE that many teachers have resigned in 2020 due to various reasons and COVID19 pandemic. Also, many schools had resorted to allocating more classes to teachers of Class 9 to 12 in order to compensate for the resources. As a result, many teachers were not available for external examiner duty as assigned. The board has taken cognizance of the fact that in the absence of external examiners, many schools have resorted to conducting practical exams with an examiner which was not appointed by CBSE. Schools, it further adds, have also not updated the names of all teachers working in schools in OASIS – as mandated by the board. CBSE has pointed out to serious operational concerns over the same. Furthermore, it states that lack of teachers would impact the board’s evaluation process which would begin from May 7. As such, the board has directed all Principals to update the data as required. Schools are required to update the information about teachers between April 5 and April 10. Failing to do so, the board would take the necessary action as the Affiliation Bye Laws and Examination Bye-laws. The board may also levy a personal penalty of Rs. 50,000 on the Principal of the school. Furthermore, the board has also warned that it ‘will not declare their (the schools’) Board’s result’. In the notice, the board has said that ‘practical conducted by an examiner not appointed by CBSE will be cancelled and CBSE will re-conduct the practical of these candidates under its supervision.’ The responsibility of complying with the order rests with the Principal of the schools. Also, the board would be drawing teachers from schools on the basis of number of students sponsored by schools for Class 10, 12 board exams 2021.
